Diploma In Laboratory Technician(CMLT)

Duration of the Course : 3 Years
Medium of Instruction : English/Nepali
Affiliated to : Council for Technical Education & Vocational Training
Qualification : Completion of SLC/SEE

Academics

The primary focus of the professional curriculum of the College of Diploma In Laboratory Technician(CMLT) is the three years (3 years), full-time academic and clinical degree program leading to the education that includes leadership skills,communications and community service.

Curriculum Overview

Diploma In Laboratory Technician(CMLT) is a healthcare career that combines advanced laboratory science with modern medical care. Analytical analysis performed in clinical laboratories by medical laboratory technologists assists physicians in both the diagnosis and the treatment of pathological conditions.Medical laboratory technologists perform and interpret a wide variety of tests, ranging from simple blood glucose tests to advanced molecular diagnostic assays.

The Diploma In Laboratory Technician(CMLT) curriculum prepares medical laboratory technologists that demonstrates professional competency in the clinical laboratory science field, including but not limited to the clinical areas of hematology, immunohematology, clinical chemistry, clinical microbiology, urinalysis, immunology/serology, and laboratory management.

While completing their Laboratory Technician courses students in the Diploma In Laboratory Technician(CMLT) program gain “real world” experience in a variety of clinical settings.

Rules & Regulations in Lab

  • Wearing of Apron is compulsory while entering in the lab.
  • Every glassware, instruments, equipments should be handled with precautions using necessary protective measures.
  • Do not use any instrument, equipments with your naked hand without the instructor approval.
  • Laughing, eating, talking, using mobile are strictly prohibited inside the lab.
  • No students are allowed to touch/handle any instrument beside their work.
  • Every glassware should be cleaned and kept in appropriate place after their use.
  • Working table should be well managed, neat and clean.
